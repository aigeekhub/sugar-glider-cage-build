CRITICAL PRE-WORK (DO THIS NOW — 5 MINUTES MAX)
─────────────────────────────────────────────

□ Clear work surface (at least 8' × 6' flat ground)
□ Lay out ALL materials
□ Have PVC cutter, measuring tape, marker ready
□ Wear safety glasses (PVC dust/debris)
□ Ensure PVC glue is ROOM TEMP (cold glue = weak bonds)
□ Test-fit one joint BEFORE full assembly


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 1: MATERIAL CUTTING PLAN (20 MINUTES)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHAT YOU'RE BUILDING:
- Four vertical corner posts (PVC pipes)
- Top & bottom rectangular frames (PVC pipes + fittings)
- Mesh screening attachment points
- One swing-open access door


EXACT PVC CUTTING SPECS (MEASURE TWICE, CUT ONCE):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────

VERTICAL POSTS (4 total):
  Size: 2" diameter PVC
  Length: 32" each
  Quantity: 4 pieces
  Cut angle: STRAIGHT (90°, no bevels)

HORIZONTAL FRAME - LENGTH (Front & Back):
  Size: 1.5" diameter PVC
  Length: 48" each
  Quantity: 4 pieces (2 top, 2 bottom, front pair + back pair)

HORIZONTAL FRAME - WIDTH (Left & Right sides):
  Size: 1.5" diameter PVC
  Length: 32" each
  Quantity: 4 pieces (2 top, 2 bottom, left pair + right pair)

INTERNAL BRACING (CRITICAL for structural integrity):
  Size: 1" diameter PVC
  Length: 22" each
  Quantity: 4 pieces (horizontal cross-braces, one per wall interior side)
  Position: 16" from bottom (middle height)

OPTIONAL CLIMBING PERCHES:
  Size: 0.75" diameter PVC
  Lengths: Cut 3 pieces at 24" each, 3 pieces at 16" each
  (These install AFTER main frame, within 30 mins before gliders arrive)


MESH SCREENING:
  Type: Plastic mesh screening (window screen material)
  Total needed: ~60-70 sq ft (to cover 4 walls)
  Calculation: (48+32+48+32) × 32" height = ~5,120 sq inches ÷ 144 = ~35 sq ft
  (Get extra for overlap and mistakes)

ZIP TIES:
  Length: 8" (standard cable ties)
  Quantity: 60-80 pieces minimum
  (You'll use them every 4-6 inches around mesh perimeter)


CUTTING SEQUENCE (FASTEST METHOD):
──────────────────────────────────
1. Cut all 2" vertical posts first (4 cuts, ~3 min)
2. Cut all 1.5" horizontal pieces (8 cuts, ~5 min)
3. Cut all 1" bracing pieces (4 cuts, ~3 min)
4. Cut optional perches (6 cuts, ~4 min)
5. Cut mesh screening into 4 wall panels (~5 min)
   - Front: 48" × 32"
   - Back: 48" × 32"
   - Left: 32" × 32"
   - Right: 32" × 32"


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 2: FRAME ASSEMBLY — STEP BY STEP (40 MINUTES)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ASSEMBLY ORDER (This sequence prevents mistakes):

STEP 1: BOTTOM FRAME ASSEMBLY (10 minutes)
───────────────────────────────────────────

This creates the cage base. Work on flat ground.

Layout:
  - Place 4 vertical 2" posts on flat surface, forming a rectangle
  - Posts position: corners of 48" × 32" rectangle
  - Corner spacing: Exactly 48" × 32" apart (measure carefully)

Connect bottom:
  1. Apply PVC glue to one end of a 1.5" horizontal piece (48" length)
  2. Insert into T-joint at bottom of front-left post
  3. Insert other end into T-joint at bottom of front-right post
  4. HOLD for 30 seconds (glue sets initially)
  5. Repeat for back horizontal piece (48")
  6. Connect left and right sides (32" pieces)
  7. You now have a 48" × 32" rectangular frame at the base

CRITICAL: Let joints set for 2-3 minutes before proceeding. Do NOT move yet.


STEP 2: VERTICAL POST ALIGNMENT (5 minutes)
────────────────────────────────────────────

With base frame glued:
  1. Check that all 4 posts are VERTICAL (use level if you have one)
  2. Posts should form 90° angles with base frame
  3. Use zip ties temporarily to hold posts vertical (every 8" up the posts)
  4. These temporary ties come OFF after top frame is glued

NOTE: Take 30 seconds to verify. Crooked posts = unstable cage. Fix now.


STEP 3: ATTACH INTERNAL BRACING (5 minutes)
─────────────────────────────────────────────

This is the STRENGTH LAYER. Prevents wobbling.

For each of the 4 walls:
  1. Take a 1" diameter bracing piece (22" long)
  2. Position horizontally inside the cage, 16" from bottom
  3. Glue one end to interior of left post
  4. Glue other end to interior of right post
  5. This creates a horizontal brace at mid-height

These braces run:
  - Front wall: horizontal brace between front-left and front-right posts
  - Back wall: horizontal brace between back-left and back-right posts
  - Left wall: horizontal brace between front-left and back-left posts
  - Right wall: horizontal brace between front-right and back-right posts

CRITICAL: These braces are NOT visible from outside (they're inside) but ESSENTIAL
for cage rigidity. Do NOT skip.

Let glue set 2-3 minutes.


STEP 4: TOP FRAME ASSEMBLY (15 minutes)
─────────────────────────────────────────

This caps the cage and completes the structure.

For the top frame (1.5" PVC):
  1. Apply glue to top ends of all 4 vertical posts
  2. Attach front horizontal piece (48") to front-left + front-right posts
  3. Attach back horizontal piece (48") to back-left + back-right posts
  4. Attach left side piece (32") to front-left + back-left posts
  5. Attach right side piece (32") to front-right + back-right posts

Result: Complete rectangular frame, 48" × 32" × 32"

HOLD each joint 30 seconds. Ensure top frame is LEVEL (not tilted).

Allow 3-5 minutes for glue to cure before moving.


STRUCTURAL CHECK (2 minutes):
─────────────────────────────
  ✓ Frame doesn't wobble when lightly pushed
  ✓ All corners are 90° (no diamond shape)
  ✓ Height is consistent (32" at all four corners)
  ✓ Top and bottom frames are parallel (not twisted)

If any fail: glue isn't set. Wait 5 more minutes.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 3: MESH SCREENING INSTALLATION (25 MINUTES)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PREPARATION:
─────────────
  1. Lay out first mesh panel (Front wall, 48" × 32")
  2. Position mesh at front of cage frame
  3. Mesh should cover entire front opening (post to post)

ATTACHING MESH — ZIP TIE METHOD (FASTEST):
───────────────────────────────────────────

DON'T glue mesh to frame. Use zip ties for speed + easy cleaning.

Front Wall (48" wide × 32" tall):
  1. Line up mesh along top edge of front frame
  2. Starting at left, place zip tie around frame and mesh every 6 inches
  3. Tighten each zip tie firmly (mesh should be taut, not loose)
  4. Zip ties go on OUTSIDE of cage
  5. Trim excess zip tie tails with scissors

     Pattern: [ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]----[ZT]
     (8 zip ties across 48" width, ~6" spacing)

  6. After top edge, do BOTTOM edge same way (8 more zip ties)
  7. Then LEFT edge vertically (6 ties, 5" spacing)
  8. Then RIGHT edge vertically (6 ties, 5" spacing)

Total front wall: ~28 zip ties

Back Wall (48" wide × 32" tall):
  - Repeat exact same process
  - Total: ~28 zip ties

Left Side (32" wide × 32" tall):
  - Top edge: 6 zip ties (~5" spacing)
  - Bottom edge: 6 zip ties
  - Left edge: 6 ties (vertical)
  - Right edge: 6 ties (vertical)
  - Total: ~24 zip ties

Right Side (32" wide × 32" tall):
  - Same as left side
  - Total: ~24 zip ties

TOTAL ZIP TIES USED: ~104
(You have 60-80... This means REUSE zip ties. After attaching mesh fully,
cut old ties and reuse to complete job. Reused ties still hold fine.)


MESH OVERLAP & SEALING:
──────────────────────
At corners where two mesh panels meet:
  1. Overlap by 2-3 inches
  2. Zip tie both layers together
  3. This creates a sealed edge (no gaps for escape)


TOP OPENING (VENTILATION):
──────────────────────────
LEAVE TOP OPEN. Do NOT cover with mesh.

Why: Sugar gliders need airflow. Top opening provides ventilation.
Plus it's your access point for feeding/cleaning.

OPTIONAL: Install 0.75" PVC perches across the top opening (see Part 4)


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 4: ACCESS DOOR INSTALLATION (10 MINUTES)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DOOR SIZE & POSITION:
─────────────────────
  - Location: Front wall, centered
  - Height: 20" tall × 16" wide
  - Center point: 16" from left edge, 12" from bottom

WHY THIS SIZE: Large enough for hands/supplies to reach inside, small enough
that your 2-3 gliders can't easily escape if door briefly opens.


DOOR CONSTRUCTION:
──────────────────
Simple hinged frame:

  1. Cut 4 pieces of 0.75" PVC:
     - 2 pieces at 20" (vertical sides)
     - 2 pieces at 16" (horizontal sides)

  2. Glue these 4 pieces into a rectangle frame
  3. Attach mesh screening to this rectangle (zip tie, same as walls)
  4. Result: Door panel, 16" × 20", completely meshed

  5. Mount to cage using two hinges:
     - Use simple gate hinges (hardware store, ~$3-5 for pair)
     - Mount on LEFT side of door
     - Hinges bolt to the front left vertical post

  6. Add catch/latch on right side:
     - Simple barrel bolt (hardware store) OR
     - Magnetic catch OR
     - Heavy-duty velcro strap

DOOR TEST:
  - Open/close smoothly? ✓
  - Mesh intact? ✓
  - Latch holds firmly? ✓


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 5: OPTIONAL CLIMBING PERCHES (FINAL 15 MINUTES — IF TIME ALLOWS)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Sugar gliders LOVE to climb. If you have 10-15 minutes left:

PERCH INSTALLATION:
───────────────────

Horizontal Cross-Perches (inside cage):
  1. Take 0.75" PVC perch pieces (24" lengths)
  2. Position horizontally inside cage, at varying heights
  3. Attach to left and right internal posts using zip ties
  4. Position: One at 8" height, one at 16", one at 24"
  5. Use 2-3 zip ties per perch (top, middle, bottom)

Vertical Climbing Sticks (inside cage):
  1. Take 0.75" PVC pieces (16" lengths)
  2. Position vertically along interior walls
  3. Zip tie every 6 inches to wall mesh
  4. Create 2-3 vertical paths for climbing

Sleeping Box (if time):
  1. Cut 6" diameter PVC section (about 8" long) from spare PVC
  2. Seal one end with PVC cap
  3. Add entrance hole (2" diameter, drilled on side)
  4. Zip tie inside cage, positioned 24" high on back wall
  5. Line with fleece/cloth for comfort

CRITICAL SAFETY NOTES
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GLUE VENTILATION:
  - Use PVC glue in well-ventilated area
  - Fumes are strong—open windows/doors
  - Avoid inhaling directly over glue container

SHARP EDGES:
  - After cutting, sand cut ends smoothly
  - Burrs can injure sugar glider paws
  - Run sandpaper lightly over all cut ends

ZIP TIE TRIMMING:
  - Cut all excess zip tie tails short
  - Gliders could get caught on long tails
  - Trim flush to the tie head

PVC STABILITY:
  - Don't rely on glue alone—zip ties add security
  - Check all glued joints before gliders arrive
  - Wobble = weak glue, apply EXTRA PVC glue to joints and re-clamp

MESH INTEGRITY:
  - Inspect for tears/holes after installation
  - Patch any holes with mesh patch + zip ties
  - No openings larger than 0.5 inches (escape risk)
