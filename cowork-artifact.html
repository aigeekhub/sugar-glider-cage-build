📐 Detailed Assembly Instructions

PHASE 1: Material Cutting (20 minutes)

CUTTING SEQUENCE (Fastest Method):

  1. Cut all 2" vertical posts first (4 cuts, ~3 min) - STRAIGHT cuts at 32" each
  2. Cut all 1.5" horizontal pieces (8 cuts, ~5 min) - 4×55⅜" and 4×36⅞"
  3. Cut all 1" bracing pieces (4 cuts, ~3 min) - 22" each
  4. Cut optional perches (6 cuts, ~4 min) - 3×24" and 3×16"
  5. Cut mesh screening into 4 panels (~5 min) - front/back 55⅜"×54", sides 36⅞"×54"
⚠️ Safety Note: After cutting, sand all cut ends smoothly. Burrs can injure sugar glider paws. Use sandpaper lightly over all cut edges.

PHASE 2: Frame Assembly (40 minutes)

STEP 1: Bottom Frame Assembly (10 minutes)

  1. Place 4 vertical 2" posts on flat surface forming a 55⅜" × 36⅞" rectangle
  2. Apply PVC glue to one end of first 1.5" horizontal piece (48" length)
  3. Insert into T-joint at bottom of front-left post
  4. Insert other end into T-joint at bottom of front-right post
  5. HOLD for 30 seconds - glue sets initially
  6. Repeat for back horizontal piece (48")
  7. Connect left and right sides with 32" pieces
  8. Let joints set for 2-3 minutes before proceeding

STEP 2: Vertical Post Alignment (5 minutes)

  1. Verify all 4 posts are VERTICAL (use level if available)
  2. Check 90° angles at base frame
  3. Use zip ties temporarily to hold posts vertical (every 8" up)
  4. Remove temporary ties after top frame is glued

STEP 3: Internal Bracing (5 minutes) - CRITICAL FOR STRENGTH

  1. Take a 1" diameter bracing piece (22" long)
  2. Position horizontally inside cage, 16" from bottom (mid-height)
  3. Glue one end to interior of left post
  4. Glue other end to interior of right post
  5. Do this for all 4 walls (front, back, left, right)
  6. These braces are INSIDE, not visible, but ESSENTIAL for rigidity
  7. Let glue set 2-3 minutes

STEP 4: Top Frame Assembly (15 minutes)

  1. Apply glue to top ends of all 4 vertical posts
  2. Attach front horizontal piece (48") to front posts
  3. Attach back horizontal piece (48") to back posts
  4. Attach left side piece (32") to left posts
  5. Attach right side piece (32") to right posts
  6. Ensure top frame is LEVEL (not tilted)
  7. Allow 3-5 minutes for glue to cure
✅ Structural Check: Frame doesn't wobble, all corners are 90°, height consistent, top and bottom frames parallel.

PHASE 3: Mesh Screening Installation (25 minutes)

ZIP TIE METHOD (Fastest):

  1. DON'T glue mesh - use zip ties for speed and easy cleaning
  2. Line up mesh along top edge of frame
  3. Place zip ties every 6 inches around perimeter
  4. Tighten firmly - mesh should be taut, not loose
  5. Zip ties go on OUTSIDE of cage
  6. Trim excess zip tie tails with scissors
  7. At corners, overlap mesh by 2-3 inches
  8. Zip tie both layers together for escape-proof sealing
Wall Size Zip Ties Needed
Front 55⅜" × 36⅞" ~28 ties
Back 55⅜" × 36⅞" ~28 ties
Left Side 32" × 32" ~24 ties
Right Side 32" × 32" ~24 ties
⚠️ Important: LEAVE TOP OPEN for ventilation. Do NOT cover with mesh. This is your access point for feeding and cleaning.

PHASE 4: Access Door Installation (10 minutes)

  1. Door size: 20" tall × 16" wide, centered on front wall, 12" from bottom
  2. Create door frame from 0.75" PVC (2×20" vertical, 2×16" horizontal)
  3. Glue frame into rectangle
  4. Attach mesh screening to door frame with zip ties
  5. Mount to cage using two gate hinges on LEFT side
  6. Add barrel bolt or magnetic catch on RIGHT side to latch securely
  7. Test: door opens/closes smoothly, mesh intact, latch holds firmly

PHASE 5: Optional Climbing Perches (15 minutes if time allows)

  1. Horizontal perches: Position 0.75" PVC pieces at 8", 16", 24" heights
  2. Vertical climbing: Attach 16" pieces vertically along interior walls
  3. Sleeping box: Cut 6" diameter PVC section (~8" long), seal one end, drill 2" entrance hole
  4. Zip tie everything securely in place
  5. Line sleeping box with fleece for comfort

✅ Pre-Glider Arrival Checklist

  • ☐ Frame is solid (no wobble when pushed firmly)
  • ☐ All mesh is taut (not sagging)
  • ☐ No gaps at corners or seams (escape-proof)
  • ☐ Door opens/closes and latches securely
  • ☐ Top is open (ventilation confirmed)
  • ☐ Bracing pieces intact inside
  • ☐ Zip ties trimmed (no sharp edges)
  • ☐ Structure holds weight when gently pressed on top
  • ☐ All glue joints have cured (no wet/tacky spots)
  • ☐ Water bottle installed and full
  • ☐ Food dishes ready and positioned
  • ☐ Sleeping area available (pouch or box)
  • ☐ Room temperature 65-75°F
  • ☐ Cage in quiet, low-traffic area
  • ☐ No direct sunlight or drafts

⚡ Quick Reference: If Running Behind

Drop these in order to save time (prioritize by importance):

  1. Perches (nice, not essential)
  2. Sleeping box (hang fleece pouch instead)
  3. Internal bracing (use single X-brace instead of 4)
  4. Door latch (use heavy velcro - faster)
  5. Corner sealing (single mesh layer okay)

MINIMUM FUNCTIONAL CAGE:

  • Frame (bottom + vertical posts + top)
  • Mesh on all 4 walls + zip ties
  • Open top
  • One access hole (door frame not required immediately)

Get this done first. Comfort features come later.
