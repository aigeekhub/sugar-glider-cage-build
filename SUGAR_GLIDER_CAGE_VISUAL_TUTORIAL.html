SUGAR GLIDER HABITAT

Build a Professional-Grade Cage in 120 Minutes

55⅜" × 36⅞" × 54" 2-3 Gliders 120 Minutes
01

Pre-Work Setup

⏱ 5 Minutes

Prepare your workspace and gather all materials. This simple preparation phase ensures smooth execution.

  • Clear work surface (minimum 8' × 6')
  • Lay out ALL materials on flat ground
  • Have PVC cutter, measuring tape, marker ready
  • Put on safety glasses
  • Ensure PVC glue is at ROOM TEMPERATURE
  • Test-fit one PVC joint with glue
02

Material Cutting

⏱ 20 Minutes

Cut all PVC pieces to exact specifications. Precision here prevents problems later.

  • Cut 4 pieces of 2" PVC at 54" each (vertical posts)
  • Cut 8 pieces of 1.5" PVC (4 at 55⅜", 4 at 36⅞")
  • Cut 4 pieces of 1" PVC at 25⅜" each (bracing)
  • Cut mesh screening into 4 wall panels
  • Sand all cut ends smooth (burr removal)
  • Check every cut is SQUARE (90° angle)
03

Frame Assembly

⏱ 40 Minutes

Build the cage structure in four sub-phases: bottom, alignment, bracing, and top.

  • Assemble bottom frame (55⅜" × 36⅞" rectangle)
  • Align vertical posts (all 90° angles)
  • Install internal bracing (27" height)
  • Attach top frame (match bottom dimensions)
  • Verify no wobble when pushed
  • Allow 3-5 minutes glue cure after each sub-phase
04

Mesh Screening

⏱ 25 Minutes

Attach protective mesh to all four walls using zip ties. This creates the escape-proof barrier.

  • Install mesh on front wall (55⅜" wide × 54" tall)
  • Zip tie every 5-6 inches around all edges
  • Mesh must be TAUT (no sagging)
  • Repeat for back, left, and right walls
  • Overlap mesh at all corners (2-3 inches)
  • Trim all excess zip tie tails short
  • Verify NO gaps larger than 0.5 inches
05

Access Door

⏱ 10 Minutes

Install the hinged access door for feeding, cleaning, and glider interaction.

  • Door position: 27⅔" from left, 18⅜" from bottom
  • Door size: 18⅜" wide × 33¾" tall
  • Mount hinges on left side
  • Install barrel bolt or latch on right side
  • Door mesh must be completely intact
  • Test open/close and latch function
